Reading the New Testament in a Month

I remember reading once in a biography of Watchman Nee that he had a habit of reading the entire New Testament every month. I thought at the time that this was amazing, a nearly superhuman feat of practical Christianity. And it is remarkable, but on some reflection, it really isn’t superhuman. Whenever I read a new novel (which often clock in at around 1000+ pages), I manage to plow through it in a couple weeks, at most. The New Testament books have 260 chapters, all together, and each chapter is probably on average about a page long, depending on the sort of Bible you have. So, by comparison, less than 300 pages, tops.

So, I was planning to read through the New Testament this month, and one simple way to do it would be to just read through nine chapters a day. You’ll be done in 29 days, having just read your last 8 chapters on the last day (that’s the way it rounds out; your last day you’d have just 8 chapters left if you read 9 per day the prior 28 days).

But I thought I’d like something a little more balanced than just a huge block of Gospels on the frond end followed by epistles and bookended by the Revelation of St. John. So, I looked a little further into how the chapters breakdown between books, and came up with this idea. Basically, we take the Gospels + Acts, and read 4 chapters a day of each; on the 29th day, this leaves one extra chapter, so 5 chapters on the last day. Also each day, starting with Romans through Revelation, we’ll read 5 chapters of an epistle, finishing with the last 3 chapters of the Revelation on the 29th day.

I decided to put Luke and Acts together, to keep the flow of Luke’s story to Theophilus, and leave John till the end. This has the interesting feature of having the Gospel of John and the Revelation of John being read concurrently. All said, it works out pretty well.

9 chapters a day is a chunk of reading, no question. But it’s really nothing compared to the amount of reading I do when I read a major new novel, so I don’t see any reason why it should be too difficult.

An aside; this is a reading plan, not a study plan. It’s intended purpose is to have me (or you, if you like) read throught the New Testament in a month. Yes, naturally, if you want a study into a particular book or topic, you’d need to read that specific book, or follow that topic, much slower and with much more re-reading and related study. Again, this is just a reading plan.

If it’s interesting to you, enjoy. Would love to hear how you do with it.

  2. Matt 5-8, Rom 6-10
  3. Matt 9-12, Rom 11-15

  9. Mark 5-8, 2 Cor 9-13

  11. Mark 13-16, Gal 6, Eph 1-4

  13. Luke 5-8, Phil 4, Col 1-4
  14. Luke 9-12, 1 Thes 1-5
  15. Luke 13-16, 2 Thes 1-3, 1 Tim 1-2
  16. Luke 17-20, 1 Tim 3-6, 2 Tim 1
  17. Luke 21-24, 2 Tim 2-4, Titus 1-2
